Card Text: The Democrat may put a card in his hand aside for next turn and draw a replacement card from the deck. The card placed aside is returned to the Democrat's hand before new cards are drawn.

   

Democrat play: Samizdat allows the Democrat to postpone him having to play a scoring cards by one Turn. This can enable him to improve his position. It can also be used to hold 2 Communist cards the Democrat doesn't want to trigger into Turn 7 for TST or hold 2 Democrat cards that haven't had their prerequisites met yet or are needed for Turn 10 Action Round 7.

 

   

Communist play: The later the event is triggered by the Communist player the less likely the Democrat has something to replace safely. The Democrat player will often hold problematic Scoring cards in the knowledge the Communist player might end the Turn triggering Exit Visas or Samizdat (or very rarely Brought in for Questioning). Unless you know you'd be missing out on a good country scoring that might not last next Turn Samizdat should be played for the 1 Ops.
