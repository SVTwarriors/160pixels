Democrat play: There are three ways for the Democrat player to gain the upper hand in Bulgarian Power Struggles. Firstly there are the Sajudis event, the Eco-glasnost events to gain access to the Bulgarian worker triangle where the Democrat will then need to place SPs with Ops to gain the upper hand. Secondly the more expensive and less decisive option, the “Sofia” space can be surrounded and broken or squeezed with Support Checks. The Bulgarian Intellectual and Student space will often be protected by the Helsinki Final Act event and the “Plovdiv” space can be accessed with the Toxic Waste event or if the Communist player made a mistake the Workers Revolt event. Finally Late Year there is the Union of Democratic Forces event.

 

   

Communist play: After Romania, Bulgaria is the country where during Final Scoring the Communist player is most likely to still hold Power. This is to a large extent determined by the Bulgarian worker triangle in Early Year. If the Democrat player gains access with the Sajudis event, the Eco-glasnost event and then places SPs with Ops into the Bulgarian worker triangle before the Communist player has secured it Bulgaria is as good as lost. If on the other hand the Communist player comes out on top in the Bulgarian worker triangle he can build up Bulgaria with spare Ops and Bulgarian (e.g. the The July Concept event) or Balkan (e.g. the Nepotism event) events until his control of the “Sofia” space in the West is also secured. Because of the Workers Revolt event The Communist player should be wary of moving into “Plovdiv” space without first controlling the “Sliven” space. From there bar an untimely triggering of the Union of Democratic Forces event or an unlucky Power Struggle the Communist player should be able to hold on the Power. Sadly Bulgaria is a low scoring country so many of the VPs to be had are from the Communist player retaining Power for Final Scoring.
