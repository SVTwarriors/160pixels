Card Text: Power: 3, Presence: 3, Domination: 6, Control: 9, +1 Per Battleground

   

Democrat play: If the Communist player puts up a fight in East Germany by building it up there are two ways for the Democrat to gain the upper hand in East Germany. The first revolves around the The Monday Demonstrations event if the Communist player hasn’t been able to prevent the pre-requisite St. Nicholas Church event from triggering. The The Monday Demonstrations event is even more brutal if it can be triggered with a +1-2 Ops modifier combo from a Democrat event(s) (e.g. The Sinatra Doctrine event, the FRG embassies event) while avoiding a -1-3 Ops modifier from a Communist and/or Neutral event(s) (e.g. the Prudence event, the Grenztruppen event, the Stand Fast event. The second is the breaking of Communist controlled spaces with the help of Democrat events and through superior Ops eroding the Communist player’s position until it can no longer be held and it collapses. Moving into and taking control of the “Leipzig” space is of particular interest here as it can no longer be Support Checked by the Communist player after the triggering of the ”We are the People!” event. Together with Democrat player control of the “Walter Ulbricht University” space which will often be protected by the Helsinki Final Act event threaten the “Berlin” space. If neither of these options are possible or succeed the Democrat player will need to discard the the East Germany Scoring card where possible and choosing the path of least resistance focus on the other countries on the map.

 

   

Communist play: Holding onto East Germany can be very expensive, hard and sometimes impossible for the Communist player considering the many Democrat events effecting East Germany. Some of these Democrat events have an immediate and significant impact, foremost being the The Monday Demonstrations event, the ”We are the People!” event and the My First Banana event, while others slowly erode the Communist players position in East Germany (e.g. the New Forum event, the Genscher, the Austria-Hungary Border Reopened event, the “The Wall Must Go!” event,…). Even if the Communist player fortify East Germany sufficiently to withstand this onslaught the Democrat player might still be able to discard the East Germany Scoring card one or multiple times if he draws it thereby denying the Communist player the VPs for Dominating or Controlling and Holding on to Power in East Germany. This expensive choice to commit to building up East Germany will by it’s very nature mean letting Power in other countries slip away.
