Card Text: Opponent -1 Ops for the rest of the turn, to minimum of 1.

   

Democrat play: The Democrat player should play the Prudence card for the 4 Ops. Normally (bar the Honecker event) the maximum number of Ops the Communist player could lose by the Democrat player triggering the Prudence event on Action Round 1 is 6 Ops. But because the Communist player will likely have card(s) of 1 Ops value, Scoring Cards and cards he could and would play for the events (that don’t refer to the Ops value of the card) it is unlikely the Democrat player will be able to get 4 Ops worth of damage out of the Prudence event.

 

   

Communist play: The Communist player should mostly play the Prudence card for the 4 Ops. Because there are more Democratic events using the Ops value of the card and one more opponent Action Round is effected there are some rare situations where the Prudence event is worth triggering by the Communist player. For example Turn 3 the Communist player can sometimes know exactly how many Democrat cards he and the Democrat player is holding with event text referring to the Ops value of the card (e.g. Gorbachev Charms the West, Consumerism, Hungarian Democratic Forum, Walesa, …) and how many 1 Ops card, Scoring cards or justifiably triggerable events the Democrat player is holding. This involves card counting of course. Middle Year and Late Year this becomes more difficult though as there are many more Democrat cards the Democrat player can justifiably trigger. If clinging on to power in East Germany is essential for the Communist player and the Monday Demonstrations event will (likely) be triggered this Turn the Prudence event can be triggered instead of or together with the Grenztruppen or the Stand Fast event to alleviate the effect of the five support checks of the Monday Demonstrations event. If the Democrat player cannot counter at least (e.g. the Sinatra Doctrine event and the FRG Embassies event). The same also applies to other Democrat events like the Union of Democratic Forces event in Bulgaria.
