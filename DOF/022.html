Card Text: Power: 3, Presence: 3, Domination: 6, Control: 9, +1 Per Battleground

   

Democrat play: The Democrat player should never let the Communist player retain Power in Poland 2 or more times because of it’s Power Value of 3. This can done by keeping up the pressure in Poland by SP placement and Support Checks through Ops or events (e.g. the Inflationary Currency event, the Walesa event, the Soviet Troop Withdrawls event…), events that influence the Power Struggle directly (e.g. the Roundtable Talks event, the Yakovlev Counsels Gorbachev event…), discard the Poland Scoring card (e.g. the Brought in for Questioning event, the Exit Visas event and the “Foreign News Coverage” TST spot) or hold it Turn 10 with or without the use of the Samizdat event. By the end of Early Year even considering aggressive and successful Communist player opening in Poland the Democrat player should by controlling the Polish “Catholic Church” space and the Helsinki Final Act event at the very least also be able to control the “Katowice” space, the “Krakow” space, the “Jaglellonian Univeristy” space, the “Polish Writers” space and control either the “Lodz” space or the “Wroclaw” space. These 6 Democrat player controlled spaces VS 8 Communist controlled spaces still has a fair chance of winning a Power Struggle and forms a base for future expansion in Poland by the Democrat player.

 

   

Communist play: In 1989 it is always important for the Communist player to pick his battles. Retaining Power in Poland is difficult and expensive because of Democrat events (e.g. the Inflationary Currency event, the Walesa event, the Soviet Troop Withdrawls event…) and pressure by a Democrat player with overwhelming Ops. All the Ops spent defending Poland is Ops that can’t be spent elsewhere and will de facto mean being overrun in other countries. On the other hand if the Communist is able to retain Power in Poland multiple times the VP reward is great because of it’s Power Value of 3. Because of this the Communist player should continuously re-assess how much effort he can afford to invest and commit to Poland considering his overall strategy. Sometimes this will mean voluntarily surrendering Power in Poland after the Power Struggle. Even if the Communist player decides to cut his losses in Poland and stops investing additional Ops there in an attempt to retain Power he should still cost-effectively resist Democrat player Control of Poland and keep Presence. Laying all his eggs in one basket can also mean the Communist player can miss out on a lot of VP if the Democrat player manages to Discard the Poland Scoring card.
