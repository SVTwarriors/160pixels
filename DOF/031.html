Card Text: Place 4 Democratic SPs in Intellectual spaces, no more than 2 per space.

   

Democrat play: The Democrat player will nearly always trigger the recurring Intelligentsia event. At the start of the game the Intelligentsia event will mostly be used to gain and secure both Presence in and Access to the Middle Year countries and their Student spaces through the Intellectual spaces. The Intellectual and Student spaces are not only cheap to take control of but when controlled they allow the play of up to 5 Leader cards during the countries Power Struggle. Ideally the Intelligentsia card ends up in the discard pile prior to the Turn 3 and the Turn 7 reshuffle increasing the number of times the Intelligentsia event can recur. Because of the vulnerability of the Intellectual spaces (and the neighboring Student spaces) to Support Checks where possible the Democrat player should try to trigger the Intelligentsia event after the Helsinki Final Act event is already in effect. This way the Communist player will be far less inclined to try to Support Check the Democrat player out of the Intellectual spaces. If Turn 3 after the Helsinki Final Act event is in effect the Dissidents Arrested card is in the Communist player’s hand the Intelligentsia event's 4 SPs can be spread out over 4 different empty Intellectual spaces to gain access to multiple countries while limiting the influence the Communist player can remove to 1 SP if he chooses to trigger the Dissidents Arrested event. To prevent access denial by the Communist player through other means (e.g. the Students Arrested TST Bonus, the State Run Media event etc.) the Democrat can use the Intelligentsia event’s SPs to increase the SPs on Intellectual spaces to 3 SPs (or more). This has two benefits not only does it protect against “no more than 2 per space” events and TST bonuses or events that can only remove two SPs in total and could thereby otherwise deny access, it also protects the play-ability of the Intellectual Leader cards against the Scare Tactics Wild card during the countries Power Struggle. When considering in which countries to place the SPs of the Intelligentsia event the Democrat player should consider (imminently) upcoming country scoring as well as the Michnik event and finally the protection against Support Checks by controlling the neighboring space of the SPs placed by the Jan Palach Week event and the Fidesz event. Late Year if Czechoslovakia is still being contested some of the Intelligentsia event’s SPs can be used to strengthen the Democrat player’s control of the Czechoslovakian Intellectual space to deny the Communist player a 50-50 chance of partially disarming the Civic Forum event with a Support Check with the card’s 4 Ops on the Czechoslovakian Intellectual space prior to triggering the event.

 

   

Communist play: The Communist player should hold the Intelligentsia card until Turn 3 and then TST it. This way it will only return Turn 7 or later (bar the Reformer Rehabilitated event). As it is a recurring event and it isn’t removed from the game after being triggered, triggering the Intelligentsia event will not alter the deck the players draw from. If the Communist player is forced to trigger the Intelligentsia event and the Helsinki Final Act event isn’t yet in effect the 2 Ops should be used to try to Support Check the Democrat player out. When triggering the the Intelligentsia event the Communist player should mainly consider (imminently) upcoming country scoring as well as the Michnik event, Jan Palach Week event , the Fidesz event and the Dissidents Arrested event. Late Year there could be some games all Intellectual spaces will already be sufficiently Democrat controlled to allow the COmmunist player to trigger the Intelligentsia without much/any effect to Final Scoring.
