Canceled by / No effect after Resolution of: None

 

  
Card Text: +1 VP. Place sufficient Democratic support in any Minority space for control. Allows play of The Baltic Way.

   

Democrat play: The Sajudis event is the first event of the 4 Baltic chain events (e.g. the Sajudis event, the The Baltic Way event, the Breakaway Baltic Republics event and the Kremlin Coup! event), that are recorded on the “USSR stability track” on the right of the 1989 map board. Before the Turn 7 reshuffle the cumulative number of VPs to be gained, the control of the minority spaces in Bulgaria and Romania and the modifier on the on Malta Summit event roll all make the Sajudis event worth triggering as opposed to using the Sajudis card for 2 Ops. After the Turn 7 reshuffle much depends on which events could still trigger. Early in the game the Sajudis event’s SPs are best placed in the “Razgad” space in Bulgaria, a Middle Year country. It also protects “Ruse” space against Support Checks after the Eco-Glasnost event is triggered. The downside of choosing the “Razgrad” space is the Bulgarian Turks Expelled event of course but the Systematization event can be used in Romania against the Harghita/Covasna space and it really isn't worth focusing on Late Year country Romania too early.

 

   

Communist play: Considering the Communist player will most of the time have higher priority cards to hold (e.g. the Helsinki Final Act card, the St. Nicholas Church card, etc.) the Sajudis card will often end up being TSTed. There will be games however that even for the TST the Communist player will have more pressing discards than the Sajudis card. So whenever possible the Communist player shouldn’t trigger the Sajudis event. Else the location of the Eco-Glasnost card, which also gives the Democrat player access, needs to be considered and if the Communist is one step ahead of the Democrat player in getting the upper hand in the Bulgarian worker triangle. In Middle Year the Sajudis event is best only triggered after The Baltic Way is in the discard pile in the hope the Baltic event’s won’t come out in the right order after the Turn 7 reshuffle or even better not at all considering the Reformer Rehabilitated event etc.. Sadly the Communist player will often have bigger fish to fry. Late Year the Communist player will often reap what he has sown with the Malta Summit unless the Baltic chain has gotten all the way to the Kremin Coup! event.
