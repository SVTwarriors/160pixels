Card Text: +1 Ops value for all cards played by the Democratic Player for the rest of this turn.

   

Democrat play: The The Sinatra Doctrine event is the Democrat player's clone of the Perestroika event and should also only be played for the event if the Democrat gets at least 4 Ops worth. There are quite a few Communist Middle Year events that suddenly become very triggerable at +1 Ops. The Democrat player has quite a few interesting events in Middle Year that do not refer to the Card’s Ops value however so if it isn’t possible for him to get a good “yield” the Turn the Democrat player draws the The Sinatra Doctrine card and he has nothing else to hold, he could hold The Sinatra Doctrine card. The The Sinatra Doctrine event strengthens the The Monday Demonstrations event significantly. The Communist player could trigger the Grenztruppen event in response though.

 

   

Communist play: The The Sinatra Doctrine event should be triggered Action Round 7 by the Communist player to get it out of the game and only give the Democrat 1 bonus Ops. (or better still Action Round 8 thorugh the triggering of the Honecker event or when the Democrat player’s hand size is reduced twice or more.) Whenever triggering the The Sinatra Doctrine event the Communist player should remember never to aggravate the The Monday Demonstrations event's 5 Support Checks. It is best for the Communist player not to discard the The Sinatra Doctrine event however to avoid it ending up in the Democrat player’s hand.
