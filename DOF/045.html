Card Text: Remove 5 Communist SPs from Eastern Europe, no more than 2 per space.

   

Democrat play: The Democrat player can severely weaken the Communist players position in Eastern Europe with the Soviet Troop Withdrawls event if the Communist player hasn’t overprotected the target spaces in advance. The Communist player will mostly not have a lot of Ops at his disposal (usually 3 Ops) to repair the damage of the Soviet Troop Withdrawls event resulting in spaces where the Communist player’s control is broken. The Communist player has only a maximum of 3 Communist or Neutral 4 Ops cards in the whole 1989 deck. If the Communist player is lucky the Perestroika event is in effect or he can trigger a Democrat 4 Ops card with little effect and thereby use the 4 Ops to repair the damage of the Soviet Troop Withdrawls event. (e.g. The Solidarity Legalized event in a fully controlled Poland) This difficulty of the Communist player to repair the damage of the the Soviet Troop Withdrawls event allows the Democrat player to either follow up with a Support Check, place SPs into the broken space or score the country changing the facts on the ground of a previously solid looking Communist player position. If the Soviet Troop Withdrawls card is drawn before the Turn 7 reshuffle and there is no strong Communist player position left in Eastern Europe that the Democrat player wants to erode he can play the Soviet Troop Withdrawals event for the Ops keeping it in the Game. On Turn 10 AR 7 the Soviet Troop Withdrawals event can be used to seriously impact Final scoring. The Soviet Troop Withdrawls event is particularly useful for the Power Value 3 countries East Germany and when the Communist has invested a lot of Ops to secure a stronghold there.

 

   

Communist play: By the time the Communist player draws the Soviet Troop Withdrawls card the Democrat player will often already be Holding Power in two Eastern European countries. This limits the spaces where the Democrat player can use the Soviet Troop Withdrawls event to contest the Eastern European countries where the Communist player still Holds Power and in turn allows the Communist player to have a better idea of which critical spaces to overprotect to prepare for his triggering of the Soviet Troop Withdrawls event. The damage done by the Soviet Troop Withdrawls event can then be repaired by the 4 Ops without the Communist players position becoming vulnerable. The Communist player will not be able to overprotect every space and might therefore allow the loss of some ground in the Eastern European countries deemed to be less important strategically. Mainly if advancing on the TST is a high priority or less likely the Communist is holding no better TST candidates the Soviet Troop Withdrawals card is often dumped on TST by the Communist player.
