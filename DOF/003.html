Card Text: Place 4 Democratic SPs in any spaces(s) in Poland. The Democratic Player may them make 2 Support Checks in Poland using the Ops value of this card. Requires Solidarity Legalized to be played as an Event.

   

Democrat play: The Walesa event, the follow-up event to the Solidarity Legalized event, further weakens the Communist player’s position in Poland. If the Communist player hasn’t yet lost or surrendered Power in Poland after the Solidarity Legalized event was triggered the Walesa event will probably make him do so. From Turn 3 to Turn 6 if the Democratic Player has already taken Power in Poland the Walesa card can be played for the 3 Ops keeping it in the deck. After turn 7 the Event can be used by the Democrat player to effect Final Scoring by taking Control in Poland, a Turn 10 Action Round 7 triggering denying the Communist Presence or else it can lay the groundwork for a successful roll on the Shock Therapy event.

 

   

Communist play: If the Solidarity Legalized event hasn’t been triggered yet the Communist Player can simply play this card for the 3 Ops. This makes Walesa it a good candidate for a Turn 1 Action Round 1 play by the Communist player. Once Solidarity Legalized has been triggered there are multiple things to consider. Firstly, has the Democrat already taken Power in Poland? Secondly, does the Democrat get +1/-1 for theWalesa event for the Support Check this Turn? (e.g. the The Sinatra Doctrine event, the FRG Embassies event, the Prudence event, the Stand Fast event) Thirdly, will the 4 SP & 2 Support Checks likely make the Communist players position implode significantly impacting Final Scoring. Fourth, the Shock Therapy event. The 3 Ops won’t repair the damage done by the Walesa event but sometimes in Middle Year the Communist player will have bigger worries than his position in Poland and it’s Final Scoring considerations and be inclined to trigger the Walesa event nonetheless.
