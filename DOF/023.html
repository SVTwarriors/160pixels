Card Text: Power: 1, Presence: 1, Domination: 2, Control: 4, +1 Per Battleground

   

Democrat play: Because the Power Value of Hungary is only 1 the Communist player retaining Power in Hungary multiple times isn’t as problematic for the Democrat player. The Democrat player should still avoid the Communist player taking Control of Hungary by at least controlling the “Szeged” space. Normally though the Democrat player has a slight edge in over the Communist player in the Hungarian Tatabanya-Miskolc-Budapest worker triangle through initial SP and the Democrat events and it will be the Communist player that is on the back foot in Hungary.

 

   

Communist play: If by looking at his hand Turn 1 the Communist player can already determine Poland is lost he can try a full out aggressive opening in Hungary in an attempt to take Control of the Hungarian Tatabanya-Miskolc-Budapest worker triangle. Else It is damage control which involves cost effectively balancing the number of Democrat player controlled spaces in such a way to retain Power in Hungary for Final Scoring or have Hungary slip away as painlessly as possible. Whichever he decides the Communist player should try to make the most of the Nagy Reburied event and the Pozsgay Defends the Revolution event to limit the cost of consolidating/losing Power in Hungary while being beware of the Hungarian Democratic Forum event.
