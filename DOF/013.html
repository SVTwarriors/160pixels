Card Text: For the rest of this turn the Democratic Player must reveal the card he will play this Action Round before the Communist player plays his card.

   

Democrat play: The Democrat player should trigger the Stasi event in Action Round 7. If the Honecker event isn't in effect the Stasi event will then trigger without any effect. Better still is for the Democrat player to trigger the Stasi event through the Brought in for Questioning event or the Deutsche Marks event for 3 or 4 Ops respectively Action Round 7. This is only possible if the Democrat player’s hand size isn’t reduced by any other events. If the Communist player triggers the Stasi event the Democrat player is advised to immediately play any Power struggle card he cannot discard with the Brought in for Questioning event. The Communist player will be able to cause mayhem otherwise with Early Year events like the State Run Media event or the Factory Party Cells event or the Gorbachev Charms the West event or (specific to Hungary) the Nagy Reburied event. If the Democrat player is holding the Common European Home event when the Stasi event is triggered he should be sure to read the following clarification. Clarification: When Stasi in effect, if the Democrat wishes to play Common European Home to cancel a Communist event, he must designate the card with the Communist event as the card he will play next, and then on his Turn announce the usage of Common European Home to cancel the Communist event. If instead the Democrat declares Common European Home to be the card he will play next, he must play it for Operations points.

 

   

Communist play: When the Communist player triggers the Stasi event he should do this Action Round 1 of course. Factors that lead the Communist player to trigger the Stasi event are, the Communist player is unable to hold the Stasi card into the next Turn, the Communist player has no other strong AR1 move, the Communist player is holding State Run Media and/or Factory Party Cells and/or Gorbachev Charms the West, the Communist player wants to force the Democrat player to play a Power Struggle card in Action Round 1 that he cannot discard with the Brought in for Questioning event, the Democrat player isn't holding the Common European Home card. The Democrat player will normally react to the Action Round 1 triggering of the Stasi event by immediately playing a Power Struggle card he is holding to avoid giving the Communist player a heads up allowing the Communist player to alter the situation on the ground and thus effecting this countries Power Struggle and Scoring. If the Common European Home card could be in the Democrat player's hand the Communist player should be sure to read the following clarification. Clarification: When Stasi in effect, if the Democrat wishes to play Common European Home to cancel a Communist event, he must designate the card with the Communist event as the card he will play next, and then on his Turn announce the usage of Common European Home to cancel the Communist event. If instead the Democrat declares Common European Home to be the card he will play next, he must play it for Operations points.
