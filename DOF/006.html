Card Text: The democratic Player must randomly discard a card. If it is a Communist event it takes place immediately.

   

Democrat play: For the Democrat player the Brought in for Questioning event is without a doubt the best recurring Communist event. The Brought in for Questioning event allows the Democrat to trigger low Ops Communist cards like Stasi for 3 Ops in Action Round 7. But more importantly it enables the Democrat player to discard problematic Power Struggle cards avoiding a painful scoring, a core part of Democrat strategy. If in Turn 2 (or Turn 6) the Democrat player is in a tight spot in country that is still in the deck he might want to hold on to the Brought in for Questioning card. Only rarely when there is a Communist card the Democrat player really wants to hold on to, as to avoid triggering it, is the Brought in for Questioning event disruptive and even then it can quite easily be TSTed.

 

   

Communist play: The Communist player will usually play the Brought in for Questioning card for Ops. If however the Democrat player has triggered the Common European Home event the Brought in for Questioning event can give the Communist player a back-to-back Action Round relatively cheaply which can allow him to set up for an Action Round 1 Support Check the following Turn. A back-to-back Action Round can also be achieved in combination with the Deutsche Marks event but is way more expensive. By counting cards the Communist player can get a better idea of which cards the Democrat player is holding (certainly Turn 3 and Turn 7) allowing him to better evaluate the “random” discard for the 3 sacrificed Ops if he would choose to trigger the Brought in for Questioning event.
