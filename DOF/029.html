Card Text: Place 6 Democratic SPs in the Charles University space.

   

Democrat play: Unless the Czechoslovakia Scoring event could be effected he Democrat player shouldn't trigger the Jan Palach Week event until the Helsinki Final Act event is active. It is also best if the Democrat player has control of the “Czech Writers” space and the Communist player doesn’t have control of the “Praha” space prior to triggering the Jan Palach Week event because else the “Charles University” space is quite vulnerable to Support Checks certainly without the protection of the Helsinki Final Act event. The Intelligentsia event can be used to retake control of the “Czech Writers” space if it has been emptied by the Dissidents Arrested event or a 2nd Support Check. The Communist adding 1 SP to the “Praha” space early in the game is a tell for him holding the Jan Palach Week card. When possible the Democrat players should take advantage of the Students Rally TST bonus or a Democrat event that allows him to play the Jan Palach Week card for 2 Ops.

 

   

Communist play: The Communist player should try to trigger the Helsinki Final Act and Support Check the Democrat player out of the the “Charles University” space before the Helsinki Final Act event is triggered. This can be done with the 1 Ops of the Jan Palach Week card itself. Two Support Checks at a -1 modifier have an average effect of 3.33 SP. If the Communist player takes control of the Praha space (e.g. the Apparatchiks event) or breaks control of the Democrat player in the Czech Writers space (e.g. the Dissidents Arrested event, the State Run Media event …) the +0 modifier results in an average effect of 5 SP. With both the Communist player will have a +1 modifier and have an average effect of 7 SP. With the Tear Gas event in front of the Communist player this would become 8 SP. Whenever playing a 1 Ops cards for it’s Ops the Communist player should take advantage of the Dissidents Arrested TST bonus before is canceled by the Democrat player’s advance on the TST.
