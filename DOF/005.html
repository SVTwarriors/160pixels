Card Text: In the next Action Round the Communist must discard a card and roll a die. To end the strike the total of the Ops value plus the die roll must exceed 5. The communist must continue to discard a card and roll a die each Action Round until the strike is over, though he may play a Scoring Card during the strike.

   

Democrat play: Before Turn 7 the Democrat player should play the General Strike card for 3 Ops to keep the card in the game and hopefully have it end up in the Communist player’s hand (where it is the most problematic) or have the General Strike event trigger from the discard pile with the Dash for the West event or the Reformer Rehabilitated event. From Turn 7 onwards the Democratic player should consider triggering the General Strike event himself. Here timing is important to maximize the impact of the General Strike event. Having the Communist player get stuck in the General Strike event is most likely near the end of the Turn after sustained pressure from the Democratic player. This increases the likelihood of the Communist player having exhausted all of his high Ops card. Else a more “expensive” option is using the Prudence event if the Democrat player can spare the Ops. It is always sensible for the Democrat player to wait until the Communist player has ditched his “worst” card on the TST before triggering the General Strike event. This increases the chance to disrupt the Communist player’s game plan and he will more likely be forced to discard his “better” cards. Triggering the General Strike event Turn 10 can also be interesting. Before any triggering of the General Strike event the Democrat player needs to think if he wouldn’t be throwing the Communist a lifeline to discard problematic Democrat cards. Often the Communist player will react to the Democrat player’s triggering of the General Strike event by playing any Scoring card he is holding of countries that are the likely target of the Democrat player’s pressure during his back-to-back Action Round(s).

 

   

Communist play: In almost all situations the Communist player will want to hold or TST the General Strike card. By playing this card for the Ops the Communist player is allowing the Democrat player to play (a) back-to-back Action Round(s) which can allow the Democrat player to gain access to or to significantly weaken the Communist player’s position in a region. There are some hands where the Communist player is holding multiple Democrat cards that cannot be held or TSTed, whose Ops value isn’t close to repairing/mitigating the damage of their event. In these cases triggering the General Strike event can hesitantly be considered if the Communist player has one 4 Ops or two 3 Ops Democrat cards with (a) nasty event(s) to have a 83.333%/(2x)66.666% chance resp. to get out from under the General Strike event when the pain of the Democrat player’s back-to-back Action Round(s) becomes too much. The Stasi event being in effect also helps in this regard. In the case the Communist player goes down this path should remember to trigger the events that are key to his strategy that are in his hand before triggering the General Strike event to avoid having to discard them later.
