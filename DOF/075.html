Card Text: The Democratic Player may discard any cards in his hand and draw an equal number of replacement cards from the deck.

   

Democrat play: The Exit Visas event allows the Democrat player to discard Communist cards (and or low 1 Ops Democrat cards he doesn't want to play for the event). If this is done Turn 7 and later the cards should never return. If the Democrat player doesn't have enough cards worth discarding with the event it could be a good idea to hold onto Exit Visas until he has a worse hand. Also one of the few events that let's the Democrat player discard a scoring card for a country that is hopeless. When the Communist player triggers the event near the end of a Turn the Democrat player should watch out not to overreach. He should never replace more than the following: Democrat Action Rounds Left this Turn - Scoring cards in Democrat hand + 1 (Held Samizdat) +1 (Held Brought in for Questioning) <= Maximum No of Scoring cards drawn The "Maximum No of scoring cards drawn" either "the No of cards you choose to discard with Exit Visas" or "The No of Scoring cards left in the Deck" (considering reshuffle) depending which is smaller.

 

   

Communist play: Another card the Communist player can weaken by playing it on his Action Round 7. Before deciding to trigger the Exit Visas event in this way the Communist player should try to figure out if the Democrat is holding a Scoring Card for a hopeless country from the Democrat's point of view. If he is it might be a good idea to hold Exit Visas when possible instead of throwing away good VPs by letting him discard the Scoring card. In these situations TST is also an option if there aren't higher priorities, certainly Turn 7 and later when there is no way it could still end up in the Democrat player's hand.
