5, Tiananmen Square Track

    5.1, Basics

 

   

The Tiananman Square Track (refered to as TST) has two core aspects. On the one hand the TST is a way for both players to discard problematic cards of their opponent without triggering their event. On the other hand if a player is able to get and/or stay ahead of his opponent on the TST or reach a TST spot first he can gain an advantage with the “Permanent” or “One Time Use” TST bonuses. His opponent can then try to catch up with TST rolls or the Tank Column/Tank Man event to cancel these “Permanent” bonuses. In a game either mainly the Discard aspect or both the Bonus and the Discard aspects of the TST will matter.

This depends on the following aspects:
- Triggering of the Li Peng event early on
- Triggering of the Tank Column/Tank Man event early on
- Triggering the The Chinese Solution event is still possible
- Triggering the Reformer Rehabilitated event is still possible
- The number of successful TST rolls early on
- Passing the Students Dispersed spot early on (COMMUNIST PLAYER ONLY)

Once a Turn each player may discard any one non-Power Struggle card to make a TST roll (his own or his opponent's), this regardless of there being a chance of success. The Tank Column/Tank Man event doesn't count as a TST roll.
Even though clearly explained in the rule book all possible TST roll modifiers are listed below.

TST roll modifiers:
- The player is TSTing his own faction's card: +1
- The player failed his previous TST roll: +1
- TST Bonus Reformer Memorialized/Reformer Discredited: +1
- TST Bonus Students Rally/Students Dispersed: +1 (1 OPS CARDS ONLY)
- The Li Peng event: +1 (COMMUNIST PLAYER ONLY)
- The Prudence event: -1
- The Perestroika event: +1 (COMMUNIST PLAYER ONLY)
- The The Sinatra Doctrine event: +1 (DEMOCRAT PLAYER ONLY)

Only two events are dependent on a player's relative or absolute position on the TST track. These are Reformer Rehabilitated and The Chinese Solution respectively.
First in Chapter 5.2 the individual TST spots are explained in more detail and a score from 0 to 6 is given to their bonus for each player. Then in Chapter 5.3 there is a TST candidate ranking for each period deck.
