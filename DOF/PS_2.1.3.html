2, Power Struggle

    2.1.3, Presence

 

   

Having and keeping Presence going into a Power Struggle in a country where there is a chance (even if it is remote) to win is a must! This first space a player controls in a country at the start of a Power Struggle allow him to draw 6 Power Struggle cards. This alone means a 6/52 chance to draw Tactic Fails with a shot (albeit remote at times) to win the Power Struggle. When a player is himself playing the scoring card of a country with an utterly hopeless Power Struggle having no Presence can be a blessing in disguise though. It can allows a player to lose with only a +1 Die Roll Modifier for the opponent. After the Turn 7 reshuffle (bar the Domino Theory event) for the Communist player this can mean, a 1/3 chance to retain Power, no Support Loss, spending the Ops elsewhere, and a smaller VP loss from the Democrat player's roll on the Victory Points table. In the Communist sacrifices the 1-3 VPs for Presence (depending on the countries Power Value) that he wouldn't have received anyway because of the Democrat player's roll on the Support Loss table he would've had to endure.
