Chain of Events: Solidarity Legalized → Walesa

 

Canceled by / No effect after Resolution of: None

 

  
Card Text: Place a Democratic SP in every uncontrolled Worker and Farmer space in Poland. The Communist player may no longer make Support Checks in Gdansk. Allows play of Walesa.

   

Democrat play: There are three main reasons the Solidarity Legalized event should be triggered by the Democratic player as soon as possible. Firstly, it allows him to take full advantage of a higher number of uncontrolled worker and farmer spaces in Poland. Secondly, it protects Gdansk from a Communist Support Check. Thirdly, it allows the Walesa event to trigger. If the Solidarity Legalized event is triggered Turn 1, Action Round 1, it often changes the course of the game by leading to Communist player to voluntarily give up Power in Poland allowing him to focus his Ops elsewhere instead of fighting the losing battle. By the end of Turn 3 depending on if the Walesa card is still in the deck and if the Communist player still (confidently) holds Power in Poland the event should often still be triggered even if it means sacrificing 4 Ops as to prime the deck. Middle Year the Democrat player will need to keep up the pressure in Poland with Walesa in combination with the many “Eastern Europe” and “Where the Opponent Holds Power” events to prevent the Communist player from holding on to Power multiple Scorings. Turn 7 and later only the effect on Final Scoring needs to be considered.

 

   

Communist play: The Solidarity Legalized event becomes weaker as the game goes on. By the end of Turn 3 there are often only a handful of uncontrolled worker and/or farmer spaces in Poland and the control of the “Gdansk” space has already been decided. By then (bar one Action Round and unless it was discarded with the Brought in for Questioning event) the Poland Scoring event has already been triggered at least once which means the Democrat player could’ve already taken Power in Poland making the effects of the Solidarity Legalized event far less painful for the Communist player. Also if Turn 3 the Walesa card is already in the discard pile the Communist player doesn’t need to worry about it until Turn 7 (Unless of course the Democrat is able to trigger it with the Dash for the West event or the Reformer Rehabilitated event) making it easier to play still. Generally before Turn 3 the Communist player should try to hold or TST the Solidarity Legalized card in all but his most surrenderist tactical retreats out of Poland. This as the Communist player would rather not give the Democrat player Poland Control for Final Scoring too easily/cheaply. If from Turn 3 onward the Communist player wants to retain Power in Poland no uncontrolled worker and/or farmer spaces should remain in Poland. The Communist player will then simply have to weigh the 4 Ops against the activation of the Walesa event.
