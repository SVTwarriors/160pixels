Card Text: If played in Early Year: -3VP, If played in Mid Year: -1VP, If played in Late Year: +3VP

   

Democrat play: Turn 1 and Turn 2 if well spent the 3 Ops of the Heal Our Bleeding Wounds card should give the Democrat player a tactical and strategic advantage that will over the course of the game gain him far more VPs than the Heal Our Bleeding Wounds event’s -3 VP penalty hurts him. A Turn 1 or Turn 2 discard would mean it would return by Turn 7 at the latest anyway and then still cause a (very minor) headache for the Democrat player. If however Turn 2 the Democrat player doesn’t have anywhere to spend the 3 Ops wisely and he doesn’t need to hold any other card the Democrat player can hold the Heal Our Bleeding Wounds card into Turn 3 and discard it after the Turn 3 reshuffle. After Turn 2 a discard can make an average +3.5 VP difference considering it isn’t drawn on Turn 7, isn’t triggered with the Reformer Rehabilitated event and considering a 50-50 chance that the Democrat player draws the Heal Our Bleeding Wounds card. Not having the Communist player ahead in VP going into Middle Year can also help with the 40th Anniversary Celebration event. Late Year it is an awesome card for the Democrat player to play as it gives both 3 Ops and +3VPs that by then are very important.

 

   

Communist play: Early Year the Communist player should play the 40th Anniversary Celebration event if he thinks he might achieve a -20 VP instant victory early on. The effect of shifting the VP counter to the Communist side of the VP track on the 40th Anniversary Celebration event should also be considered. Else unless it is Turn 3 the Communist player should when possible play this card for the 3 Ops. The Communist player should make sure the 40th Anniversary Celebration card isn’t in the deck after the Turn 7 reshuffle.
