Card Text: Place in front of the Democratic Player. In the next Power Struggle the Democrat randomly draws 2 Power Struggle cards from the Communist hand. Then place this card in the discard pile.

   

Democrat play: As the Roundtable Talks event is recurring the Roundtable Talks card is a candidate for the TST for the Communist player as triggering the event will not remove the card from the game. If considering the other cards that need TSTing or holding on to, the Roundtable Talks card’s Ops outweigh the 4 Power Struggle card advantage the Roundtable Talks event gives the Democrat player in the likely next Power Struggle should playing the Roundtable Talks card for 3 Ops be considered. An active the Roundtable Talks event should when possible be wasted by the Communist player on countries where he doesn’t have much of a chance to lose with anything less than a +3 Power Struggle the die roll modifier for the Democrat player. If possible the Roundtable Talks card should be discarded soon after the Turn 3 and Turn 7 reshuffle. Clarification: The two cards drawn from the Communist hand at the start of the Power struggle are added to the Democrats hand.

 

   

Communist play: As the Roundtable Talks event is recurring it is a prime candidate for TST for the Communist player as triggering the event will not remove the card from the game. Only if the tactical advantage of using the 3 Ops to place SPs in a country outweigh the 4 Power Struggle card advantage the "Round Table Talks" event gives the Democrat player should playing it for Ops be considered. Rarely can 3 Ops buy you 4 Power Struggle cards worth of advantage and often a 4 Power Struggle card disadvantage will lose you Power of a country. If possible discard after the Turn 3 reshuffle.
Clarification: The two cards drawn from the Communist hand at the start of the Power struggle are added to the Democrats hand.
