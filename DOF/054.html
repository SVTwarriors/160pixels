Canceled by / No effect after Resolution of: None

 

  
Card Text: After the players draw cards for the next Power Struggle in Romania, the Democrat draws 15 Power Struggle cards and takes 1 Action Round in Romania using Ops equal to 3 x the number of rally cards drawn. These 15 cards are then discarded. Allows play of The Tyrant is Gone.

   

Democrat play: The Democrat player should remember that the The Crowd Turns Against Ceausescu event doesn’t take place at the moment of it’s triggering, after triggered the The Crowd Turns Against Ceausescu event the The Crowd Turns Against Ceausescu card is placed in front of the Democrat player and the execution of the event text only takes place at the start of the Romania scoring event if/when it occurs. As Late Year (and the Romania scoring card) draws nearer the Democrat player should consider triggering the The Crowd Turns Against Ceausescu event himself as opposed to the playing the The Crowd Turns Against Ceausescu card for 3 Ops. The The Crowd Turns Against Ceausescu event relies on a good draw during the Power Struggle to be worth sacrificing the points. The probabilities of each draw are shown in the table and graph below.


Usually the Democrat player will be in a weak position in Romania often centered around the “Harghita/Covasna” space and/or the Romanian Student and Intellectual spaces and/or the “Timisoara” space, but sometimes being completely locked out altogether. There is a 22% chance of a 3, 4, 5 or 6 Rally in the Square draw which can give the Democrat player two 9, 12, 15, 18 Ops Support Checks respectively. The immense strength of these high Ops value Support Checks can wipe out the Communist player, even from very strong positions, and significantly impact the Romania Scoring event by flipping the control of spaces and establishing strongholds for the Democrat player for Final Scoring considerations. The effect of the more likely Support Checks of a (66% chance) 1-2 Rally in the Square draw depends more on the target spaces’ stability, the modifier(s) and of course the Support Check roll(s). Instead of trying a weak Support Check with a low chance of success the Democrat player can also break Communist control of spaces by placing SPs with the The Crowd Turns Against Ceausescu event’s Ops to deny Romania Domination, Romania Control, Control of one space or Control of a space type. The Democrat player should the The Tyrant is Gone event that triggers at the end of the Romania Scoring event if it is in front of him. The Democrat player should be sure to have access to (even after losing the Power Struggle and considering Scare Tactics), and even better a good modifier on, the space he has the Ceausescus flee to so he can collect the VPs for capturing them.

 

   

Communist play: The The Crowd Turns Against Ceausescu event is completely safe to trigger by the Communist player after the Democrat player has taken Power in Romania or if it is no longer possible to trigger the Domino Theory event and the Romania scoring card is in the discard pile or in Turn 10 if Romania scoring can no longer happen through Democrat player discards. In other situations the Communist player probably won't want to trigger the The Crowd Turns Against Ceausescu event there are situations where his hand forces him to though. The most favorable of these is the Communist player having such a strong a position in Romania and being protected by the National Salvation Front event and/or the Securitate event while the 3 Ops of the The Crowd Turns Against Ceausescu card are needed elsewhere. After a successful the The Crowd Turns Against Ceausescu event, events like Systematization and Ceausescu can repair damage to ensure the Communist player keeps Romania Domination or Romania Control for Final Scoring. Late Year, unless the Communist players knows the Democrat player isn’t holding the Romania Scoring card, the Communist player should try to play the The Tyrant is Gone card prior to triggering the The Crowd Turns Against Ceausescu event to defuse it.
