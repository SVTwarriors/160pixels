New Year's Eve Party*

   

  

Player: C

  

Period: LY

  

Ops: 2

  

Type: 1x

  

Res: Imm.

  

Dur: Turn

  

Country: None

  

Space: None

  

SP: Y*

  

SC: N

  

VP: P

  

PS: P

  

TST: N

  

HS: N

  

Chain of Events: Not part of a Chain

 

Canceled by / No effect after Resolution of: None

 

  
Card Text: The Communist may declare the game will end at the end of this turn. If the Communist has power in 4 or more countries -3 VP, if 3 or fewer countries +3 VPs. This takes place immediately. At the end of the turn the Democrat may choose 1 county where the Communist still holds Power to have a final Power Struggle. There is no Final Scoring.

   

Democrat play: If the Communist doesn’t have Power in 4 or more countries and he doesn’t have big VP lead he will probably not declare the game over making New Year’s Eve Party a Free 2 Ops. Else if things are starting to go the Democrat player’s way and many countries are about to topple TST.

 

   

Communist play: The Communist player should only play this Event for an automatic -20VP win or on his Last Action Round if he has a significant VP lead considering the New Year’s Eve Party VPs. The VP lead is needed because the Democrat player could in his last Action Round seriously worsen the prospects of the Communist player in the New Year’s Eve Party Scoring country or play a VP event resulting in a Democrat win. With the Honecker event the Communist player could calculate a worst case outcome of the New Year’s Eve Party Scoring guaranteeing a win by playing the New Year’s Eve Party event. If it is played the New Year’s Eve Party event should always be played in the last Action Round to not give the Democrat additional Action Rounds to prepare. The Democrat player isn’t obliged to choose a country for New Year’s Eve Party Scoring.
